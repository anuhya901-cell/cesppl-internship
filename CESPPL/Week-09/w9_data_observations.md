# Week 9 – Data Observations

## 1. BIN LIFTING
- Garbage trucks lifting large waste bins using hydraulic mechanisms.
- Workers are often present near the vehicle.
- Main visual cue: bin being lifted into the truck.

## 2. BIN WASHING
- Bins are being cleaned using water and washing equipment.
- Wet bins and cleaning machinery are common.
- Main visual cue: washing process instead of lifting.

## 3. GATE MEETING
- Groups of workers gathered near gates or depot entrances.
- Mostly people standing and discussing work.
- Main visual cue: group meeting without operational activity.

## 4. LFC
- Images mainly show landfill operations and waste disposal areas.
- Heavy vehicles and accumulated waste are commonly visible.
- Main visual cue: landfill environment.

## 5. MANUAL BEACH CLEANING
- Workers cleaning the beach using hand tools.
- Sand and sea are visible in most images.
- Main visual cue: manual labor without machines.

## 6. MECHANICAL SWEEPING
- Road sweeping performed using specialized sweeping vehicles.
- Sweeping machine is the dominant object.
- Main visual cue: mechanical road sweeper.

## 7. MECHANIZED BEACH CLEANING
- Beach cleaning performed using tractors or cleaning machines.
- Large equipment operates on sandy areas.
- Main visual cue: machinery on the beach.

## 8. PRIMARY COLLECTION
- Waste collection directly from residential or commercial areas.
- Collection vehicles and bins are common.
- Main visual cue: waste collection from the source.

## 9. ROAD SWEEPING
- Roads cleaned using workers with brooms or simple tools.
- Urban roads and streets are the main background.
- Main visual cue: manual sweeping on roads.

## 10. SECONDARY VEHICLES
- Vehicles transporting waste between facilities.
- Large transport vehicles are common.
- Main visual cue: transportation rather than collection.

---

# Similar Class Comparisons

- **Manual Beach Cleaning vs Mechanized Beach Cleaning:** Hand tools vs cleaning machinery.
- **Mechanical Sweeping vs Road Sweeping:** Sweeping vehicle vs workers with brooms.
- **Bin Lifting vs Bin Washing:** Lifting mechanism vs washing equipment.
- **Primary Collection vs Secondary Vehicles:** Waste collection vs waste transportation.

---

# GATE MEETING Observations

- This class is visually distinct because it focuses on groups of people rather than waste management activities.

---

# LFC Observations

- Most images contain landfill surroundings with waste piles and heavy vehicles, making the class different from other operational activities.

---

# Shortcut Risks

- Some images contain date/time stamps or camera overlays.
- A few images include GPS or watermark information.
- These overlays could become shortcut features during model training.

---

# Unusual Images

- A few blurry or low-light images were observed.
- Some images contain multiple activities in the same frame.
- A small number of images may require label verification.

---

# Ten Key Observations

1. Bin lifting images consistently show hydraulic lifting mechanisms.
2. Bin washing images contain visible water and cleaning equipment.
3. Manual beach cleaning relies on workers using hand tools.
4. Mechanized beach cleaning is identified by heavy machinery.
5. Mechanical sweeping images are dominated by sweeping vehicles.
6. Road sweeping mainly shows manual cleaning activities.
7. Primary collection focuses on collecting waste from source locations.
8. Secondary vehicles mainly transport waste between facilities.
9. Gate meeting images are visually different because they show worker gatherings.
10. Some images contain overlays such as timestamps or GPS information that could influence model learning.
